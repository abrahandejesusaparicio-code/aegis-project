// Operational Sequence

How the Ravens Work

Every mission follows a precise 6-step autonomous loop — from launch to data delivery — with zero human input required.

01
🛫

Auto Launch

The Principium HMI triggers a scheduled or on-demand patrol. Ravens lift off autonomously from their docking pad — no pilot needed.

02
📡

Mesh Activation

As Ravens ascend they instantly become LoRaWAN relay nodes — extending the private network deep into valleys and behind hills where ground signal fails.

03
🤖

AI Scanning

YOLOv8 processes the live camera feed in real time — detecting, classifying, and geotagging waste by type, volume, and urgency score.

04
🗺️

Map Generation

Scan data is compiled into a live waste heatmap and pushed to the Principium HMI — technicians see exactly where Havocs need to deploy.

05
📲

Havoc Dispatch

Optimized routes are pushed to Havoc units via the mesh network. Ravens stay airborne to maintain signal coverage for the entire ground mission.

06
🔋

Auto Return & Recharge

At 20% battery Ravens autonomously return to the Principium dock, land with precision, and recharge in 25 minutes — ready for the next sortie.

// Extended Range Infrastructure

Antenna Relay Network

Fixed ground-based relay antennas extend the Ravens' operational range far beyond their onboard LoRaWAN capacity — allowing the fleet to explore deeper zones, farther hillsides, and previously unreachable areas of San Miguelito without breaking mesh connectivity.

Ground Relay · LoRaWAN Extension Node

// How It Works

A

Raven broadcasts to antenna

As the Raven pushes into deep terrain it hands off its LoRaWAN signal to the nearest fixed relay antenna, maintaining an unbroken data link.

B

Antenna relays to Principium

The ground antenna forwards scan data, route commands, and telemetry back to the Principium hub in real time — even when the Raven is kilometres beyond its base range.

C

Chained antennas = unlimited reach

Multiple antennas can be chained across hillsides and rooftops — each one extending the network further, effectively giving Ravens unlimited exploration range across the entire district.

// Antenna Specifications

  • Type:LoRaWAN 868/915 MHz omni-directional repeater
  • Range:Up to 15 km per node in open terrain
  • Power:Solar panel + 12V LiFePO₄ backup battery
  • Install:Pole-mount — rooftop, hilltop, or streetlight
  • Weatherproof:IP67 — fully sealed against rain and humidity
  • Backhaul:4G LTE uplink to Principium if fiber unavailable
