// Charging Infrastructure

Inductive Charging at the Principium

Principium · Fleet Charging Dock

When a Havoc unit completes its mission or hits 15% battery, it autonomously navigates back to the Principium hub and docks on its inductive charging pad — no cables, no manual handling, no downtime. While recharging, the pneumatic vacuum system simultaneously extracts the bio-slurry from Slot A and the CIP system flushes the internals with ozonated water. By the time the battery is full, the robot is clean and ready.

  • 01

    Auto-dock alignment

    Havoc uses ultrasonic sensors to align precisely on the inductive pad within ±3cm accuracy.

  • 02

    Simultaneous unload + charge

    Bio-slurry extraction, CIP sanitation flush, and inductive charging all happen in parallel — the 90-minute cycle covers all three.

  • 03

    Fleet cycling — zero gaps

    With 5 Havoc units staggered across the district, there is always at least 3 active in the field while 2 cycle through the dock — continuous 24/7 coverage.

// Operational Sequence

How the Havoc Works

From dispatch to dock — a full autonomous mission cycle with zero human handling at any stage.

01
📡

Route Received

The Havoc receives an optimized waste-collection route from the Ravens via the LoRaWAN mesh network — no human dispatch required.

02
🦾

Terrain Navigation

Spider-Tracks engage — the Havoc climbs stairs, crosses uneven ground, and navigates narrow veredas that block every other vehicle.

03
🗑️

Waste Pickup & Sort

NIR spectroscopy and sensors identify waste type on contact. Organics go to Slot A (macerator), recyclables to Slot B (compactor) — automated at the point of pickup.

04
⚙️

Onboard Processing

The 2HP macerator grinds organic waste into bio-slurry paste while the 3,000N compactor crushes plastics and metals — processing happens in motion, not at a facility.

05
🔋

Auto Return & Dock

At 15% battery or full capacity, the Havoc autonomously returns to the Principium dock for simultaneous recharge, slurry extraction, and CIP sanitation.

06
♻️

Slurry to Biotank

The extracted bio-slurry is piped directly into the Algae Biotank where Chlorella vulgaris converts it into biodiesel, methane, and fertilizer — closing the loop.
