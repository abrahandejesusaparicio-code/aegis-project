// Energy Revenue Streams

Three Outputs, One Loop

The Biotank doesn't just process waste — it generates three distinct commercial revenue streams from a single input, making Aegis economically self-reinforcing.

⛽ Revenue Stream 01

Biodiesel

Algae lipids are extracted and converted via transesterification into B100 biodiesel — a drop-in fuel that powers the Havoc-Heavy long-range transport units and can be sold to the district's public transport network.

~58,700 L/ha/yr
rendimiento — 10× el biodiésel de soja
🔥 Revenue Stream 02

Methane

Residual algae biomass after lipid extraction undergoes anaerobic digestion, producing biogas (methane). This gas is combusted on-site to generate electricity that fully powers the Principium hub — lights, sensors, pumps, HMI, and cooling.

100% Hub Power
Principium completamente autoalimentado
🌱 Revenue Stream 03

Fertilizer

The nitrogen and phosphorus-rich digestate left after methane extraction is processed into agricultural-grade liquid fertilizer and sold to Panamanian farms and urban gardens — creating a direct economic link between the city's waste and local food production.

// The Closed Loop

How the Biotank Closes the Loop

Phase III · Circular Economy Engine
01

Havoc delivers bio-slurry

After each mission, Havoc docks at the Principium. The pneumatic vacuum system extracts the organic bio-slurry from Slot A and pipes it directly into the Biotank.

02

Algae feeds and grows

Chlorella vulgaris absorbs the nutrients from the slurry and CO₂ from the air and methane combustion loop — converting waste into dense biomass continuously, 24/7.

03

Harvest every 6 hours

A centrifuge extracts the dense algae paste from the reactor every 6 hours. Lipids go to the biodiesel press, residual biomass goes to the anaerobic digester.

04

Three products leave the hub

Biodiesel fuels the fleet. Methane powers the hub. Fertilizer is sold to agriculture. CO₂ from methane combustion loops back into the reactor — nothing is wasted.

05

Carbon credits registered

Every kilogram of CO₂ sequestered by the algae is logged automatically via the Principium HMI and submitted to the Verra VCS carbon registry — generating additional monthly revenue.
